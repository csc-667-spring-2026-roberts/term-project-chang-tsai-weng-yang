Gin Rummy Rules

This page explains how to play Gin Rummy in a clear, practical way. You can use it as a quick reference while playing in our app.

1. Objective

Form valid card groups (sets and runs) and minimize your deadwood points. Win rounds by knocking or going gin, and win the game by reaching the target score first.

2. Basic Terms

  • Set: 3 or 4 cards of the same rank (for example, 8-Hearts, 8-Clubs, 8-Spades).
  • Run: 3 or more cards in sequence of the same suit (for example, 5-6-7 of Hearts).
  • Deadwood: Cards not used in any set or run.
  • Knock: End the round with deadwood at or below the knock limit.
  • Gin: End the round with zero deadwood.

3. Setup

  • Two players use a standard 52-card deck.
  • Each player receives 10 cards.
  • One card is placed face up to start the discard pile.
  • The remaining cards form the stock pile.

4. Turn Flow

  1. Draw one card from either the stock pile or discard pile.
  2. Rearrange your hand to build sets/runs and reduce deadwood.
  3. Discard exactly one card to end your turn.

A hand should always return to 10 cards at the end of your turn.

5. Ending a Round

Knock

You may knock when your deadwood is at or below the table limit (commonly 10 points). After knocking, both hands are revealed and scored.

Gin

If all cards are arranged in valid groups and deadwood is zero, you declare gin. This usually gives a bonus on top of deadwood difference.

6. Scoring Basics

  • Ace = 1 point
  • 2-10 = face value
  • J, Q, K = 10 points each

The round winner typically gains the difference between both players' deadwood totals.

7. Undercut (Common Rule)

If the knocker does not have lower deadwood than the opponent, the opponent scores an undercut bonus plus deadwood difference.

8. Winning the Game

Play multiple rounds. Keep a cumulative score. The first player to reach the target score (often 100) wins the game.

  • Use Draw Stock or Draw Discard to draw.
  • Select a card and use Discard to end your turn.
  • Use Sort to organize your hand faster.
  • Press Knock when your hand is ready to close the round.
